Social Engineering
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------


+ Social Engineering Concepts
+ **Can you define Social Engineering for us?**
  - Hacking the user
  - Traditional hacks are exploits against vulnerabilities in software or systems
  - Here we manipulate the user into performing actions
+ **What level of damage could a successful Social Engineering attack cause?**
  - Damage levels are the same as a conventional hack


+ Social Engineering Techniques
+ **How does someone pull off a Social Engineering attack?**
  - It can be done...
    + In person
    + Electronically
      - Computer and mobile
+ Tactics to gain compliance
  - Authority
  - Scarcity
  - Social proof
  - Urgency
  - Likeness
  - Fear
+ Shoulder surfing, Dumpster diving, eavesdropping
+ USB key drop
+ Tailgating vs Piggybacking
  - Piggybacking = Manipulate someone into letting you bypass security
  - Tailgating = User unwittingly allowing unauthorized person through security
